Covid-19 Total number of confirmed cases

Though the first case of Covid-19 was reported back in 2019, it has started impacted from mid March, 2020 around the world. Till 03/09/2023 US, India, France, Germany and Brazil have most number of confirmed cases.

This line graph helps to find out the pattern of growing number of Covid 19 confirmed cases for those 5 countries

It has five lines one for each country. Color legends are added for visual affordance. The graphs load in the page in an animated way.

Based on the trendline graph showing the confirmed COVID-19 cases for the top 5 countries (US, India, Germany, France, Brazil), here are some detailed observations:

1. United States (US): The US shows a steep and continuous increase in the number of confirmed cases throughout the timeline, peaking significantly around the end of 2020 and early 2021. There is a notable spike around January 2022, indicating the impact of the Omicron variant, which caused a sudden surge in cases. Subsequent to this spike, the case growth rate slows down but continues to increase steadily.

2. India: India’s case numbers start to rise significantly around the middle of 2021, aligning with the emergence of the Delta variant. There is a sharp increase in cases around April 2021, which corresponds to the middle of the 3rd wave caused by the Delta variant. After this peak, the trend flattens out significantly, indicating effective control measures and/or a decline in new infections.

3. Germany: Germany shows a gradual increase in confirmed cases throughout 2020 and 2021. There are noticeable spikes towards the end of 2021 and the beginning of 2022, which could be attributed to the spread of the Delta and Omicron variants. Post these spikes, the curve flattens, suggesting a stabilization in the number of new cases.

4. France: France's trendline indicates a steady increase in confirmed cases with a notable acceleration starting around October 2020, which aligns with the 2nd wave in the country. There is a significant rise in cases towards the end of 2021, coinciding with the impact of the Omicron variant. The trend continues to rise but at a slower pace post this spike.

5. Brazil: Brazil’s trend shows a steady rise in confirmed cases with two notable spikes: one around November 2020 (3rd wave) and another around January 2022 due to the Omicron variant. After these spikes, the curve indicates a more gradual increase, suggesting a reduction in the rate of new infections.
